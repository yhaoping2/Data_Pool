
⚫️ General Multi-Format Repositories
Best for finding a mix of CSV, JSON, PDF, and sometimes images in one place.

Kaggle Datasets
https://www.kaggle.com/datasets
--- Best for: CSV, JSON, SQLite, Image archives (ZIP).
--- License Tip: Use the filter on the left side: "License: CC0: Public Domain" to ensure copyright safety.

Zenodo
https://zenodo.org/
--- Best for: Academic datasets which often include "messy" real-world files like PDF reports, Excel sheets with macros, and raw instrument data.
--- License Tip: Look for the "Access Right: Open" tag and check the license metadata on the right sidebar (often CC-BY or CC0).

Data.gov** (USA) /** **** **Data.gov.uk (UK)** 
http://data.gov/
--- Best for: Government transparency reports (PDF), Census data (CSV/XLSX), and geospatial data (JSON/XML).
--- License Tip: Most works created by the US Federal Government are in the public domain.

⚫️ Document Formats (PDF, DOCX, PPTX, XLSX)

GovDocs1 / Digital Corpora
https://digitalcorpora.org/corpora/file-corpora/files/
--- Best for: PDF, DOC, DOCX, PPT, PPTX, XLS.
--- Details: This is a massive corpus of nearly 1 million files scraped from .gov domains. It is specifically designed for file format research. You can download smaller zip files (e.g., just 1000 files) instead of the whole terabyte.
--- Warning: Because these are real files scraped from the web, scan them for malware; however, they are excellent for "real-world" file processing tasks.

SafeDocs (via Digital Corpora)
https://digitalcorpora.org/corpora/file-corpora/cc-main-2021-31-pdf-untruncated/
--- Best for: PDFs (specifically untruncated, diverse PDFs).
--- Details: A cleaner subset derived from Common Crawl, focusing on PDF structure.

⚫️ Video & Audio (MP4, WAV, MP3)

Wikimedia Commons
https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Main_Page
--- Best for: Images, Video (WebM/MP4), Audio (OGG/WAV).
--- License: Almost exclusively Creative Commons or Public Domain.
--- Tip: Search for categories like "Videos of nature" or "Instructional videos."

Internet Archive ( Archive.org)
https://archive.org/
--- Best for: Old movies (MP4), Audio books, Historical texts.
--- License: Filter by "Media Type: Movies" and look for "Public Domain" or "No Rights Reserved" collections (like the Prelinger Archives).

Pexels Videos** /**** Pixabay**
https://www.pexels.com/videos/
--- Best for: High-quality stock footage (MP4) and images.
--- License: Their custom license usually allows free use without attribution, which is safe for RL tasks.

⚫️ Specialty Formats (SVG, JSONL, Code)

SVG Repo
https://www.svgrepo.com/
--- Best for: SVG icons and vectors.
--- License: Most are licensed under CC0 (Public Domain).

GitHub
https://github.com/
--- Best for: Source code (.py, .js), JSONL, Markdown (.md).
--- Tip: Search for "awesome-public-datasets" repositories. Always check the repository's LICENSE file (look for MIT, Apache 2.0, or Unlicense).

Hugging Face Datasets
https://huggingface.co/datasets
--- Best for: JSONL, Parquet, Text corpora.
--- Tip: This is the standard for NLP text data. You can filter by task (e.g., "Video Classification" to find video datasets).
